The Sun

The Sun is a 4.5-billion-year-old yellow dwarf star holding 99.86% of our solar system's total mass. Roughly 1.3 million Earths could fit inside it, with a core temperature of 27 million°F. It is mostly hydrogen and helium plasma that takes ~8 minutes to reach Earth, shining for another 5 billion years

The Meteors

Meteors, or "shooting stars," are not stars but space debris—mostly dust and rock—that burn up upon entering Earth's atmosphere at speeds up to 160,000 mph. Millions enter the atmosphere daily, with roughly 33 metric tons of material hitting Earth daily, mostly in the form of pebbles. They are often leftovers from comets

The Moon

The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite, formed billions of years ago by a massive collision. It is gradually drifting away by 3.8 cm every year, while its surface experiences extreme temperatures from \127°C to -173°C. Because it rotates at the same speed it orbits, we only ever see one side
